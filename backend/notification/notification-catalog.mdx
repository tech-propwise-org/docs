<Note>
**Source of Truth:** This catalog is derived from `notification-type.enum.ts`, `notification-type.registry.ts`, and domain listeners under `src/modules/notification/listeners/`, plus external emitters (AI agent, escalation, distribution cron, workflow engine, reminder scheduler).
</Note>

## Conventions

<Info>
**Sender Convention:** The "Sender" is the acting user. In most bulk notification paths, the sender is filtered OUT of the recipient list unless the caller explicitly opts into sender delivery.

**Channels:** Registry candidates determine available channels; user preferences and per-reminder overrides decide the final delivery channels.

**Actions:** Lists the in-app notification actions available to recipients:
- `navigate` = opens the linked entity
- `approve`/`reject`/`rsvp_*` = in-app-only actions that change notification status
- Email sends entity deep-link buttons for `navigate` actions only
- Interactive actions (approve/reject/RSVP) require the recipient to open the app
</Info>

### Categories & Subcategories

**Categories:**
- `properties` (formerly `real_estate`)
- `crm`
- `access` (formerly `team`)
- `system`
- `messaging`

**Subcategory** is derived from type and never stored in the database. See `NotificationSubcategory` enum.

### Setting Visibility

<Warning>
Some notification types carry a `visibilityPermissions` list in the registry. When present, `NotificationPreferenceService.getPreferences()` filters those types out of the preference catalog **before** sending it to the frontend.

Users who do not hold any of the listed permissions do not see the toggle row in the UI. This is **cosmetic only** — delivery logic is unchanged (stakeholders still receive the notification). See the permission gate table at the end of this document.
</Warning>

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Category" icon="address-book">

  <Card title="Subcategory" icon="user-plus">
    `leads`
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

| Type | When Sent | Sender | Recipients | Why | Priority | Actions |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|-----|----------|---------|
| `lead_assigned` | A lead is assigned to a user/team | Assigner | New stakeholders (excl. assigner) | Notify assignees of new lead | MEDIUM | View Lead |
| `lead_unassigned` | A lead is returned to the pool | Performer | Former stakeholders (excl. performer) | They lost the lead | MEDIUM | — |
| `lead_claimed` | A user claims a lead from the pool | Claiming user | Other stakeholders | A pool lead was taken | MEDIUM | View Lead |
| `lead_stakeholder_added` | A user/team added as lead stakeholder (non system-initiated) | Adder | Added user | Informed of assignment + commission | MEDIUM | View Lead |
| `lead_stakeholder_removed` | A stakeholder removed from a lead | Remover | Removed user (not self) | Informed of removal | MEDIUM | — |
| `lead_stakeholder_commission_changed` | A lead stakeholder's commission % changes | Changer | Affected user | Commission updated | LOW | View Lead |
| `lead_primary_assignee_assigned` | A user or team-pool row is made primary on a lead | Changer | Affected user, or each team leader when the new primary is a team pool (not self) | Now primary owner | MEDIUM | View Lead |
| `lead_primary_assignee_unassigned` | A user or team-pool row loses primary on a lead | Changer | Affected user, or each team leader when the former primary was a team pool (not self) | No longer primary | MEDIUM | View Lead |
| `lead_primary_assignee_row_changed` | Primary assignment moves between stakeholder rows (same user) | Changer | Affected user | Their primary slot moved | MEDIUM | View Lead |

<Note>
**System-initiated stakeholder creations** (distribution/escalation engines) emit `lead_assigned` instead of `lead_stakeholder_added` to avoid duplicate/confusing pings.
</Note>

<Tip>
**Grouped Notifications:** `lead_stakeholder_commission_changed` is grouped — all lead-commission changes to a user within a **1-minute window** collapse into one in-app notification + one digest email (`groupKeyFields: []`, `mode: digest`). 

The digest header counts **distinct leads** (by `entityId`), not rows. When a single change touches a recipient twice on the **same** lead (e.g. a team leader whose personal cut drops while their team-pool cut rises) the digest reconstructs **one concise sentence** from the item payloads:

`{actor} updated commissions on {lead} — your share: A% → B%, {Team}: C% → D%.`

Never shows "2 leads"; falls back to the raw item messages if payload fields are missing. Per-item messages use the arrow form `{actor} updated your commission on {lead}: A% → B%.`
</Tip>

## 5. CRM — Activity Stakeholder Inbox (Granular)

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Category" icon="address-book">
    `crm`
  </Card>
  <Card title="Subcategory" icon="calendar-check">
    `activities`
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Triggered when an activity is logged/scheduled on a lead or contact. 

**Sender:** The performer  
**Recipients:** Lead + linked-contact stakeholders with `team.crm.view`/`team.crm.manage` (excl. performer)  
**Priority:** All MEDIUM  
**Channels:** IN_APP + EMAIL candidates

### Activity Types

- `activity_call_logged`
- `activity_email_logged`
- `activity_sms_logged`
- `activity_meeting_scheduled`
- `activity_site_visit_scheduled`
- `activity_note_added`
- `activity_marked_important`

**Actions (all types):** View record (navigates using the payload `entityType` / `entityId`)

<Info>
**Triggering Events:** Create/schedule only — editing an activity does NOT ping.
</Info>

<Tip>
**Grouped Notifications (Digest):** These types are the **pilot** for the generic grouping engine. A burst of activity logged on the same lead/contact for a stakeholder collapses into **one** in-app digest + **one** digest email per **15-minute fixed window** (`groupKeyFields: ['entityId']`, `mode: 'digest'`) instead of N separate pings.

Grouping is keyed per notification type, so e.g. several `activity_call_logged` on one lead group together.
</Tip>

## 26. Notification Priorities

Priority levels determine urgency and default delivery channels:

| Priority | Description | Default Channels | Use Case |
|----------|-------------|------------------|----------|
| **CRITICAL** | Requires immediate attention | IN_APP + EMAIL + SMS | Security alerts, system emergencies |
| **HIGH** | Important, time-sensitive | IN_APP + EMAIL | Approvals, invitations, overdue tasks |
| **MEDIUM** | Standard notifications | IN_APP + EMAIL | Assignments, stage changes, comments |
| **LOW** | Informational only | IN_APP | Minor updates, archives, deletions |

<Note>
User preferences can override default channels for each notification type (except CRITICAL, which always delivers via all channels).
</Note>

## 27. Grouped Notifications (Digest Mode)

<Info>
**Grouping Engine:** Certain high-volume notification types support **digest mode** — multiple notifications of the same type for the same entity (or across entities) collapse into a single digest notification within a time window.
</Info>

### Grouping Parameters

| Type | Group Key | Window | Mode | Header Logic |
|------|-----------|--------|------|--------------|
| `lead_stakeholder_commission_changed` | `[]` (per recipient) | 1 minute | digest | Counts distinct leads, consolidates same-lead changes |
| `deal_stakeholder_commission_changed` | `[]` (per recipient) | 1 minute | digest | Same as lead commission |
| `activity_*` (all types) | `['entityId']` | 15 minutes | digest | Groups by entity (lead/contact) |
| `comment_added` | `['entityId']` | 10 minutes | digest | Groups by record |

### Digest Header Examples

<CodeGroup>
```text Commission Changes (Same Lead)
{actor} updated commissions on {lead} — your share: 10% → 15%, {Team}: 5% → 3%.
```

```text Commission Changes (Multiple Leads)
{actor1}, {actor2} updated commissions on 3 leads
```

```text Activity Burst
{actor} logged 5 activities on {lead}: 3 calls, 1 meeting, 1 note
```

```text Comments
{actor1}, {actor2}, and 2 others commented on {deal}
```
</CodeGroup>

<Tip>
**Fallback Behavior:** If payload fields are missing or the grouping logic cannot construct a concise header, the system falls back to displaying individual notification messages in a list.
</Tip>
